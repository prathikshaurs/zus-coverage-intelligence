Pulling records is a commodity. The work is knowing where coverage is thin, why, and what to do about it - per network, per geography, per customer.
Built to the shape of the Interoperability Operations Analyst role · modeled on Zus's FHIR data-mart structure (UPID identity, DATA_SOURCE provenance, network-level coverage)
01 / COVERAGE

Where the network is strong, and where it isn't

Coverage rate is the share of expected (patient × network × data-type) records Zus successfully retrieved. Sorted worst-first, the way you'd triage it.

02 / GEOGRAPHY × NETWORK

Coverage heatmap

Each cell is the coverage rate for a state on a given network. The dark patches are where to look first - a single weak cell against a strong row usually means a connectivity or participation problem, not a sick patient population.

03 / INVESTIGATION

Flagged anomalies & root-cause hypotheses

The engine compares every cell to its network's own norm and flags statistically significant shortfalls (z ≤ -2). Each card pairs the finding with a plain-language hypothesis and a recommended operational action - the hand-off from analysis to support, solutions, or product.

04 / DATA QUALITY

Retrieved, but not usable

Coverage can look fine while quality quietly fails. These are network/data-type combinations that return records which arrive near-empty or far too slowly, caught by a separate, non-geographic scan.

05 / ADT SIGNAL

From push-alert noise to actionable events

Raw ADT notifications are noisy: duplicates within hours, alerts with no clinical context attached. This is the funnel from every alert received down to the admits and discharges a care team can actually act on.

All figures are computed from fully synthetic data generated to resemble the Zus FHIR data-mart structure (UPID identity resolution, DATA_SOURCE provenance, network-level retrieval facts). No real patient data, no proprietary Zus data, and no PHI are used anywhere in this project. Coverage gaps and anomalies are deliberately injected into the synthetic dataset so the detection logic can be validated against a known ground truth. Not affiliated with or endorsed by Zus Health.
